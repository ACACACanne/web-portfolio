About Me

I didn't begin in Code & Data!

I began in colour, in care, in the quiet choreography of service, managing purchases, healing skin, lifting boxes, lifting spirits. My hands knew how to soothe, my eyes how to read people. But somewhere between the spreadsheets and the silence, I heard a different language calling, one of Logic Loops and Luminous Possibility.Stepping into tech felt like stepping into fog. I stumbled through syntax, doubted every bracket, and wondered if creativity could live in a console. But I kept walking. I studied late, built broken things, and rebuilt them better. I learned that data has rhythm, that interfaces can breathe, and that Code, when written with care, can be a kind of poetry. Now, I build systems that speak with purpose. I design interfaces that feel like home. I analyse patterns not just to predict, but to understand. My work lives at the intersection of Precision and Soul, modular, Responsive, and quietly cinematic. This is more than a portfolio. It’s a reflection of Resilience, Reinvention, and the belief that even the most unexpected paths can lead to something beautiful.

From Beauty Rooms to Code Blocks: My Journey into Tech


Shifting from a non-technical background into computer science and data analytics wasn’t just a career change, it was a full-body recalibration. I came from worlds where Empathy, Service, and Creativity were my tools: managing purchases, running a beauty therapy business, working in warehouses. I knew how to read people, not Python. I could build trust, not Databases. When I stepped into Development, I felt like I was learning to walk again, in a language I didn’t yet speak. Every error message felt personal. Every bug whispered doubt. I wasn’t just Debugging code; I was debugging my own belief in whether I belonged here. But I kept going. I studied late into the night, rewrote broken loops, and asked questions even when I feared they sounded naïve. I learned to see beauty in logic, poetry in structure, and empowerment in data. Slowly, the fog lifted. I stopped surviving the syntax and started shaping it. Now, when I build a form, animate a scroll, or train a model, I carry all those past roles with me, the empathy of a therapist, the precision of a manager, the resilience of a warehouse worker. My journey into tech wasn’t linear, but it was deeply human. And that’s what makes my code different: it’s Written With Heart.


What’s Next?


Now, I’m building forward. My next chapter is about deepening what I’ve started, Expanding my portfolio with backend Functionality, crafting subpages that tell the story behind each project, and launching a blog where I can share what I’ve learned and what I’m still learning. I want to create tools that are not just functional, but emotionally resonant. I want to write tutorials that feel like conversations. I want to build systems that empower others the way tech has empowered me. Every new Challenge is a chance to refine my voice and Vision, not just as a developer, but as a storyteller in code. Every new project is an opportunity to connect, to create, to contribute. I’m excited for what’s next.
